Data & Models

Models were derived using symbolic regression in Eureqa. Inputs: S = free speed (km/h), K = density (veh/km), Q = observed flow (veh/hr). Model fit statistics (R¬≤) indicate very strong predictive power across all three stages.

Developed predictive equations (V = predicted speed): Before bottleneck: V = 0.176*S + 0.016*K - 1.48 - 0.00122*Q At bottleneck: V = 0.521*S + 0.000887*Q - 1.61 - 1.02e-5 * S*Q - 5.54e-8 * Q^2 After bottleneck: V = 0.992*S + 0.439*K - 38.71 - 1.29e-6 * Q^2 - 0.00127 * K^2

Key Insights

  • Sharp pre-bottleneck deceleration ‚Äî vehicles reduce speed ~200‚Äì300 m before the critical point, typically due to roadside activities and boarding/alighting behavior.
  • Severe speed drop at bottleneck ‚Äî the narrow bottleneck segment shows the lowest mean speeds (~31.9 km/h), confirming queue formation at local chokepoints.
  • Non-linear recovery after bottleneck ‚Äî speed recovery follows an exponential-like trend; full normalization occurs around ~500 m downstream in typical conditions.
  • High explanatory power ‚Äî R¬≤ > 0.97 for all models indicates robust models suitable for planning and near real-time estimation.
